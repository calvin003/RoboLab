The capstone project

What is
Micromouse?

An IEEE engineering competition since 1977. Students build a tiny autonomous robot that maps and solves a 16×16 cell maze as fast as possible. No remote control. Just your code, your hardware, your algorithms.

Unit 6 · 8 weeks

The Micromouse
build schedule

Each student builds their own mouse — no teams, no shortcuts. Individual design, individual race time.

Wks 1–2
Chassis design & PCB layout

CAD a two-wheeled chassis in Fusion 360. Design or modify a custom PCB in KiCad — motor drivers, IR sensor array, MCU footprint. Order boards for fabrication.

Wks 3–4
Electronics & wiring

Solder components, wire up IR sensors (left, right, front), wheel encoders, DC motors, and the microcontroller. Bench-test each subsystem before integration.

Wks 5–6
Flood-fill algorithm

Implement flood-fill in Python or C++. Simulate on a software maze first, then run on the classroom 8×8 practice maze. Debug wall detection and cell updates.

Wks 7–8
Speed tuning & Race Day

PID-tune motors for maximum safe speed. Optimize the return run after mapping. Compete on the official 16×16 race maze — fastest qualifying time wins.

Curriculum projects

What they build
along the way

Unit 3 · Electronics
Line Following Robot

IR sensor array + PID control loop. Tracks a taped line at increasing speeds. First real closed-loop control system students build.

Unit 4 · Programming
Obstacle Avoider

Ultrasonic ranging + state machine logic. Navigates a cluttered room autonomously. Introduces real-time decision loops in Python.

Unit 5 · Navigation
Maze Solver

Right-hand rule, then A* pathfinding on a live-mapped grid. Direct precursor to Micromouse — students graduate naturally to the full 16×16.

Unit 6 · Capstone
Micromouse

The full capstone: custom chassis, soldered PCB, flood-fill algorithm, and a timed race on an official 16×16 maze. Every unit in the course feeds into this.

Unit 2 · Mechanical
Custom Robot Chassis

Full CAD design in Fusion 360, 3D printed, assembled by hand. Students specify tolerances, pick fasteners, and revise based on load testing.

Unit 3 · Electronics
Encoder Odometry Bot

Wheel encoders + dead reckoning to navigate precise distances and angles without GPS. Foundational for any closed-loop mobile robot.
